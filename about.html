Our Purpose

We created 17 Ways to provide a range of technology services to the Australian market. As a small independent company with a wealth of experience across a range of technologies and business areas, we can provide advice and services to your business, whatever problems you may face. We know that technology can be quite daunting. Our aim is to simplify it and help you to make the right decisions.

Our Background

The senior partners of 17 Ways are Tim, Mark and John. We began working at Macquarie Bank in 1997. Over the next 17 years we worked across all business lines within Macquarie which included living and working in Australia, New Zealand, Europe, Asia, the Middle East and the United States. We worked on a wide range of activities including infrastructure design and operations, architecture, as-a-Service solutions, company mergers, joint ventures, multi-year development programs, technology and organisational change and offshoring. Prior to Macquarie we each had IT careers in industries as diverse as health care, insurance and government.
Our experience, skills and contacts allow us to deliver the right outcomes for our clients.

Our Name

The number 17 comes from the number of years that we all spent at Macquarie. The compass logo and the name 17 Ways reflects our experience and our ability to look beyond the standard offerings and to develop a solution that best meets our customer needs. This approach is reflected in our slogan: Options. Solutions. Results.

Our Management Team

John Tufts

Senior Partner

John has over 20 years experience across all aspects of IT Infrastructure, including operational, engineering, service management and business-facing account management roles. John has led teams in systems integration and technology delivery, 24x7 incident response and support, service management and portfolio delivery. John’s business facing roles at Macquarie Bank included responsibility for infrastructure for the Capital Markets and Funds Management groups, and included a posting in Philadelphia working on the Delaware Investments integration. Other overseas posting have included New York and Hong Kong. John’s experience also includes IT due diligence, portfolio management, engineering, finance, IT strategy and working with offshore teams. Prior to working at Macquarie, John worked as a Systems Engineer at New Childrens Hospital. He also holds a Bachelor of Business, a Diploma in Law and an Associate Diploma in Computing.

Tim Mallyon

Senior Partner

Tim has worked across a number of technology areas including application development, infrastructure engineering and operations, account management and project office. During his time at Macquarie Tim was responsible for a number of global services including x86 Server, Unix mid-range, Vblock, Database Services, Virtual Computing and Global Infrastructure Projects. Tim was responsible for delivering the first converged computing platform into Macquarie based on Vblock, and was also responsible for delivering the first ‘as-a-Service’ offerings hosting Oracle and Microsoft SQLServer. Tim also spent several years as an infrastructure account manager working directly with senior business managers and heads of business delivering solutions for areas such as Business Banking, Mortgages, Margin Lending, Real Estate, Funds Management and Equity Markets. Prior to Macquarie Tim worked as a Senior Consultant for a Sydney based solutions company. Tim holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Business.

Mark Guthrie

Senior Partner

Mark has worked in both application development and infrastructure roles. During his time at Macquarie he ran the Foreign Exchange trading systems for over a decade. Working directly with the heads of the business he rebuilt the front end systems from the ground up, set up a number of joint ventures and companies, and ran a 24x7 operations team. Mark led Macquarie's offshore development and testing capability in the Philippines for 2 years running a team of 650 staff. He also spent 2 years in Singapore migrating and running the front office technology teams. His infrastructure experience includes running Macquarie's global infrastructure operations team through Y2K and 3 years as the infrastructure account manager for Macquarie's FICC business. Prior to Macquarie, Mark worked in the UK's largest insurance company for 5 years as well as working for IBM in Australia. Mark holds a Bachelor's Degree in Physics and co-authored an IBM Redbook on software distribution.
