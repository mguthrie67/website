A man goes into a village pub which is full of locals. After a few minutes one of the locals stands up and shouts "23!". Everyone starts laughing. A few minutes later another guy shouts out "12!", and again everyone laughs. The stranger asks the barman what is going on.

"Oh we got tired of telling the same old jokes." the barman tells him, "So we numbered them. Now instead of telling the joke we just shout out the number."

"I see." says the stranger, "Let me try." and he shouts out "42!". The whole pub erupts in laughter that goes on for ages. When it finally stops he says to the barman "How come they are laughing so hard at that one?"

And the barman says "It's because they've never heard it before."

In a nutshell that joke explains data compression and de-duplication which we will come back to shortly.

Virtualisation

In A Brief History of the Data Centre we discussed how Data Centres have evolved over the past few decades. One of the most significant changes has been the move to virtualisation. By running multiple virtual machines on a single physical server comapnies have been able to reduce cost and speed up delivery considerably. The dominant product in this space is VMWare. Depending upon whose data you believe, VMWare has somewhere between 51% and 89% of the virtualisation hypervisor market. While other products such as Microsoft's Hyper-V and Citrix XenServer are slowly catching up, VMWare still consistenly comes out ahead in reviews.
