Virtual CIO

Compared to other professional services industries the IT industry is fairly immature. It is relatively easy to get a lawyer, accountant or auditor to work with your company, but it is far harder to engage a senior IT person. IT operates more like a trade than a profession. In the same way you can get a plumber or electrician called out to help you, you can get a PC engineer or a web designer quite easily in the IT space. However if you want to get someone to help with your strategy, to negotiate for you with a vendor or to review your processes and look for improvements then you pretty quickly find yourself at the high end of town dealing with an army of consultants who are looking for ways to improve their business, rather than ways to improve yours.

At 17 Ways we think there should be a better way. We offer a virtual CIO services which is much more similar to the way lawyers and accountants operate. We'll get to know your business and give you advice on what you should focus on and when you need to use our experience you only pay for the time that you need.

For more information visit our Virtual CIO Information Page.

Mergers and Separations

The merging and separating of technology as a result of corporate changes can be a daunting endeavour. The first few times that you work on one of these it can seem as if the work will never be done. Having done a large number of these over many years we are well placed to help you. By following a few simple guidelines and focusing on the most critical activities, even the largest technology marriage or divorce can be achieved.
